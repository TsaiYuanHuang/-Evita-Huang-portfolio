問題是什麼

The Problem

2025 年，台灣正式邁入超高齡社會。對於在家照顧牙口退化、行動不便長者的照顧者而言，每週採買都是一項高壓決策任務——面對琳瑯滿目的商品，他們往往難以判斷「什麼才是真正適合的選擇」。

In 2025, Taiwan officially entered a super-aged society. For family caregivers of elderly with dental deterioration or limited mobility, grocery shopping is a high-stakes weekly decision task — facing a sea of products, they often struggle to identify "what's truly the right choice."

傳統問卷研究可以指出「照顧者感到困難」，卻無法回答更關鍵的問題：困難發生在購物流程的哪一刻？為什麼會在那一刻產生猶豫或錯誤決策？

Traditional survey research can identify "caregivers feel difficulty" — but fails to answer the more critical question: At which moment in the shopping process does difficulty occur? Why does hesitation or wrong decisions happen at that specific moment?

這個缺口，促使我們重新思考研究方法本身——如果要真正理解「貨架前的 3 秒決策」，就必須設計出能捕捉那個瞬間的研究情境。

This gap led us to rethink the research method itself — if we truly want to understand "the 3-second shelf decision," we need to design a research context that can capture that exact moment.

Vibe Coding 熱力圖：將行為資料視覺化

Vibe Coding Heatmap: Visualizing Behavior Data

原始行為紀錄是大量的事件標記資料——如何讓研究者快速找到「有意義的空間模式」？我透過 vibe coding（以自然語言描述需求、快速生成分析工具的開發方式），在有限資源下建構了這套互動式熱力圖。

Raw behavioral records were extensive event-tagged data — how to help researchers quickly find "meaningful spatial patterns"? I used vibe coding — a development approach where you describe needs in natural language to rapidly generate analysis tools — to build this interactive heatmap within resource constraints.

這套工具不只是「把數字畫成顏色」，而是同時呈現三個互補的分析維度，讓研究者能從不同角度解讀同一份資料：

This tool does more than "turn numbers into colors." It simultaneously presents three complementary analytical dimensions, allowing researchers to interpret the same data from different angles:

執行過程Execution

從觀察到決策邏輯建模

From Observation to Decision Modeling

行為觀測告訴我們「發生了什麼」，但無法直接解釋「為什麼」。透過觀測後的深度訪談，我引導受訪者回溯採買歷程中的關鍵決策時刻，將行為資料與動機連結起來。

Behavioral observation tells us "what happened" — but doesn't directly explain "why." Through post-observation in-depth interviews, I guided participants to retrace key decision moments in their shopping journey, connecting behavioral data with underlying motivation.

Co-creation Workshop: Users as Design Decision Makers

這是團隊首次針對一般消費者（2C）設計的參與式驗證工作坊。目標不只是「蒐集意見」，而是讓受訪者直接在模擬情境中參與設計假設的驗證過程。

This was the team's first participatory validation workshop designed for general consumers (2C). The goal wasn't just "collecting opinions" — it was having participants directly participate in validating design hypotheses within simulated scenarios.

特別設計了三種實體教具降低抽象討論門檻：迷你貨架模型（讓受訪者實際排列產品）、質地溝通卡（對應不同食材特性）、以及命名卡（測試不同的專區命名方式）。

We designed three custom physical props to lower the threshold for abstract discussion: Mini shelf models (letting participants physically arrange products), texture communication cards (corresponding to different ingredient properties), and naming cards (testing different zone naming approaches).

反思Reflection
核心學習Key Learning
「研究的價值，不只是理解使用者，而是能夠重構使用者做決策的環境。」
"The value of research isn't just understanding users — it's the ability to reconstruct the environment where users make decisions."

在這個專案中，我觀察到許多關鍵決策發生在極短的時間內——貨架前的停留與猶豫。使用者事後未必能完整回憶或解釋當下的判斷依據，這讓我意識到：若要真正支持產品或服務決策，研究方法本身也需要被設計。

In this project, I observed that many critical decisions happened within seconds — the hesitation in front of a shelf. Users often couldn't fully recall or explain their in-the-moment reasoning afterward. This made me realize: To truly support product or service decisions, the research method itself needs to be designed.

關於資源限制的一個小故事：後來我發現國外研究機構已透過 MR 技術做更高效率的貨架 A/B testing。當下有一點衝擊，但我後來重新思考：在有限資源下，是否能用更輕量的方式達到類似的研究價值？Vibe coding 熱力圖就是這個問題的答案之一。「我學到的不是用哪個方法，而是怎麼在不同資源條件下選對方法。」

A note on resource constraints: I later discovered overseas research institutions were using MR technology for more efficient shelf A/B testing. It was a moment of surprise — but I reframed it: within limited resources, could we achieve similar research value through lighter means? The vibe coding heatmap was one answer to that question. "What I learned wasn't which method to use — it was how to choose the right method given different resource conditions."
